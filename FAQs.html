FAQs:

Q: Do I need to be a CS major to apply?

A: No. The only requirement is that you are interested in Computer Science.

Q: Will events be held in person?

A: Events during the Fall 2020 semester will be held virtually and events during the Spring 2021 semester may be held in person depending on safety.

Q: How much does participation cost?

A: Participation in BEARS is free of charge!

Q: What is the time commitment for BEARS?

A: There will be at most three events a month: 1) a 40-minute event with a CS faculty who will present her research and lessons learned from a career in CS research; 2) a hands-on tutorial in partnership with the Vagelos Computational Science Center's workshops; 3) one meeting with a group of 5 other BEARS participant and a current Ph.D. student/postdoc discussing topics related to the experience of CS research.

Q: Do I need to be available for the entire program?

A: Students who are available for all events will be prioritized, but we encourage students who can only partially participate to apply.

Q: I don't know what research in computer science is all about, would I be a good fit?

A: Yes! The workshop aims to introduce careers in computer science research to undergraduate students. We are assuming that this is all new to you!

Q: Why should I participate?

A: BEARS provides an opportunity to learn about careers in computer science research and network with peers and mentors.

Q: Who do I contact if I have more questions?

Additional questions can be directed to Dr. Adam Poliak and Dr. Sarah Morrison-Smith at bears@barnard.edu.
