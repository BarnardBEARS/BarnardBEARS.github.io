Barnard BEARS: Better, Enhance, and Advance Research Series:

A workshop series preparing Barnard undergraduate women for opportunities in Computer Science research.

About BEARS

BEARS is a new program organized by Barnard Computer Science during the 2020-2021 academic year to prepare Barnard students for successful careers in Computer Science research. By participating in BEARS, Barnard students will learn about the ins and outs of Computer Science research and academia, build a self-sustaining and supportive community of peers interested in Computer Science research, and develop a netowrk of mentors.

BEARS consists of multiple events, e.g. monthly presentations by women CS faculty and hands-on technical workshops run in partnership with the Vagelos Computational Science Center (CSC). BEARS participants will be divided into small groups based on research interests and each group will be led by a current Columbia CS Phd Student or PostDoc. These groups will serve as smaller support systems during the academic 2020-2021 year and beyond. Participants will also be encouraged to apply for summer research opportunities and Barnard, Columbia, and other insititutions.
